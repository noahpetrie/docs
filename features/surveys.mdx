Gathering stakeholder feedback is essential for understanding change readiness, measuring adoption, and identifying areas needing attention. Leap Change provides flexible survey tools for collecting and analyzing feedback.

## Survey types

### Single-question surveys

Quick pulse checks with a simple rating scale:

- Perfect for regular check-ins
- High response rates due to simplicity
- Easy trend analysis over time

### Comprehensive surveys

Multi-section assessments for detailed feedback:

- Multiple questions organized in sections
- Mix of rating scales and open-ended questions
- Deeper insights into specific topics

## Creating a survey

1. Navigate to **Surveys** in the sidebar
2. Click **Add survey**
3. Enter survey details:
   - **Name**: Internal identifier
   - **Initiative**: Link to relevant project
   - **Type**: Single question or comprehensive
   - **Question**: What you're asking (for single-question surveys)
4. Click **Create**

### Comprehensive survey builder

For multi-question surveys:

1. Add sections to organize questions
2. Create questions within each section:
   - Rating scales (1-10, 1-5, agree/disagree)
   - Multiple choice
   - Open-ended text
   - Yes/no questions
3. Set required vs. optional questions
4. Preview the survey experience
5. Publish when ready

## Distributing surveys

### Unique survey links

Generate a shareable link:

1. Open your survey
2. Click **Copy link**
3. Share the link via email, chat, or other channels

### Email invitations

Send personalized invitations:

1. Click **Send survey**
2. Select stakeholders to invite
3. Customize the invitation message
4. Set a deadline (optional)
5. Send invitations

<Note>
  Email invitations generate unique response tokens, enabling you to track who has responded while preserving anonymity of actual responses.
</Note>

## Survey status

- **Open**: Accepting responses
- **Closed**: No longer accepting responses

Toggle survey status at any time to start or stop data collection.

## Analyzing results

### Response summary

View high-level metrics:

- Total responses
- Response rate (responses / invitations sent)
- Average rating (for rating-scale questions)

### Rating distribution

Visualize how responses are distributed across the rating scale.

### Trend analysis

For recurring surveys, track how metrics change over time.

### Individual responses

Review detailed responses:

1. Click **View results**
2. Browse individual submissions
3. Read open-ended feedback
4. Export data for further analysis

## Response rate optimization

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Keep it short">
    Shorter surveys get higher response rates. Only ask what you need.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Explain the purpose">
    Tell stakeholders why their feedback matters and how it will be used.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Set deadlines">
    Clear deadlines create urgency without being pushy.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Send reminders">
    Follow up with non-respondents, but limit to 1-2 reminders.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Share results">
    When stakeholders see their feedback leads to action, they're more likely to respond in the future.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Anonymous vs. identified responses

Surveys support anonymous collection:

- Response content is not linked to specific stakeholders
- Only aggregate metrics are visible
- Builds trust and encourages honest feedback

For identified responses:

- Enable tracking for follow-up
- See which stakeholders have responded
- Useful for mandatory assessments

## Email preview

Before sending invitations, preview how the email will appear:

1. Click **Preview email**
2. Review the message formatting
3. Check that links work correctly
4. Adjust and send

## Closing and archiving

When data collection is complete:

1. Close the survey to stop new responses
2. Export results for records
3. Delete if no longer needed (removes all response data)

<Warning>
  Deleting a survey permanently removes all response data. Export your results first.
</Warning>
