Understand how **Leap Change** streamlines change impact management through automated detection, assessment, prioritization, and response workflows across the entire change lifecycle.

## How Leap Change Works

Leap Change provides an end-to-end change impact management platform that helps organizations identify change impacts early, understand who and what is affected, prioritize actions, and coordinate effective responses.

This page walks through the main phases of the **change impact lifecycle** in Leap Change and points you to where each phase lives in the product and documentation.

## Change Impact Lifecycle at a Glance

Most organizations move through a common flow:

1. Detect upcoming or in-flight changes.
2. Identify and log potential change impacts.
3. Assess impact severity, scope, and readiness.
4. Prioritize and plan change actions.
5. Execute and coordinate change responses.
6. Track outcomes and close impacts.
7. Use insights to improve future change delivery.

Each of these phases maps to specific areas in Leap Change, described below.

## Detection & Intake

Leap Change begins working as soon as a change signal is identified. These signals can come from:

- Project plans or roadmaps
- System or platform changes
- Process updates or policy changes
- Organizational or role changes
- External or regulatory drivers

You can connect or intake change signals through structured forms, integrations, or manual entry. Once captured, change signals can:

- Automatically generate draft change impacts.
- Attach to existing initiatives or programs.
- Trigger notifications or review workflows.

For more detail, see the **Change Intake and Detection** documentation.

## Creating Change Impacts

Change impacts are the core record of “what will be affected” in Leap Change. You can create them:

- Automatically from detected change signals.
- Manually through the UI during assessments or workshops.
- Via integrations with project, HR, or delivery tools.

When a new impact is created, Leap Change captures key properties such as:

- Impacted audience or role
- Business area or process
- Severity and complexity
- Timing and dependency
- Associated initiative or change source

These properties drive prioritization, planning, and reporting later in the lifecycle.

See **Creating and Managing Change Impacts** for details.

## Assess & Triage

Once an impact exists, the next step is understanding **how significant it is and what to do about it**.

From the Change Impact detail view, practitioners can:

- Assess impact severity, effort, and risk.
- Identify affected personas, teams, or locations.
- Adjust timelines as initiatives evolve.
- Add qualitative context, assumptions, and notes.
- Assign ownership for response planning.

This is also where you see the **impact timeline**, including changes, assessments, assignments, and decisions.

For more guidance, see the **Impact Assessment & Triage** documentation.

## Plan & Coordinate Responses

After assessment, Leap Change helps teams move from insight to action.

Typical activities include:

- Defining required change actions (communications, training, enablement).
- Coordinating activities across initiatives and teams.
- Assigning owners and due dates.
- Tracking dependencies and sequencing.
- Automating notifications and reminders.

Much of this is powered by **workflows** that react to impact changes (for example, “high severity impact identified” or “initiative moves to execution”).

Explore the **Workflows and Action Planning** documentation to see what’s possible.

## Execute & Track

As change actions are executed, Leap Change provides visibility into progress and coverage.

Teams can:

- Track completion of actions and deliverables.
- Monitor readiness across audiences.
- Surface risks or gaps early.
- Keep stakeholders informed with consistent updates.

This reduces reliance on spreadsheets, slide decks, and manual status chasing.

---

## Close & Learn

When impacts have been addressed, they can be closed with appropriate outcomes recorded.

At this stage, Leap Change can:

- Enforce required closure fields (for example, outcome, residual risk, lessons learned).
- Capture effectiveness feedback.
- Roll insights into reporting and analytics.

This ensures change knowledge compounds over time instead of being lost between projects.

---

## Analytics & Insights

Leap Change automatically records every impact, assessment, and action so you can answer questions like:

- Where are our highest change impacts concentrated?
- Which audiences are most frequently affected?
- Are we identifying impacts early enough?
- How effectively are actions being completed?
- Which initiatives carry the most change risk?

The **Change Analytics** documentation covers dashboards, filters, and metrics in detail.

---

## Change Impact Properties

Change impact properties are the fields that describe an impact and power automation. They can include:

- Built-in fields (severity, audience, timing, status).
- Custom fields defined by your organization (for example, department, geography, initiative type).

These properties are used to:

- Categorize and prioritize impacts.
- Drive workflow conditions.
- Enforce governance and consistency.
- Break down analytics across dimensions that matter to you.

You can manage these in **Impact Properties / Form Settings**.
