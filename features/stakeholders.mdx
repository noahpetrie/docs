Effective stakeholder management is critical to change success. Leap Change provides comprehensive tools to identify, analyze, and engage the people affected by your changes.

## Stakeholder database

Maintain a central registry of all stakeholders across your organization.

### Adding stakeholders

1. Navigate to **Stakeholders** in the sidebar
2. Click **Add stakeholder**
3. Enter the required information:
   - **Name**: Full name
   - **Email**: Contact email
   - **Role**: Job title or function
   - **Department**: Organizational unit
4. Add optional details:
   - Phone number
   - Location
   - Manager
   - External ID (for integration with HR systems)
5. Click **Save**

### Bulk import

Import multiple stakeholders at once:

1. Click **Import** in the stakeholders view
2. Download the CSV template
3. Fill in your stakeholder data
4. Upload the completed file
5. Review and confirm the import

## Stakeholder attributes

### Influence level

Rate how much power a stakeholder has over the change's success:

- **High**: Can significantly impact outcomes
- **Medium**: Has moderate influence
- **Low**: Limited direct influence

### Support level

Track their current stance toward the change:

- **Champion**: Actively supports and advocates
- **Supporter**: Generally positive
- **Neutral**: Neither supports nor opposes
- **Resistant**: Opposes the change
- **Blocker**: Actively working against the change

### Interest level

How engaged they are with the change:

- **High**: Very interested and engaged
- **Medium**: Moderately interested
- **Low**: Limited interest

## Power-interest grid

Visualize your stakeholder landscape using the power-interest matrix.

<Info>
  The power-interest grid helps you prioritize stakeholder engagement based on their influence and interest levels.
</Info>

### Quadrant strategies

| Quadrant | Strategy |
|----------|----------|
| High power, high interest | **Manage closely** - Regular engagement, keep informed of all decisions |
| High power, low interest | **Keep satisfied** - Meet their needs without overwhelming them |
| Low power, high interest | **Keep informed** - Regular updates, leverage their enthusiasm |
| Low power, low interest | **Monitor** - Minimal effort, periodic check-ins |

## Stakeholder groups

Organize stakeholders into logical groups for targeted communications.

### Creating groups

1. Go to **Stakeholders** > **Groups** tab
2. Click **New group**
3. Enter a group name and description
4. Add stakeholders to the group
5. Click **Save**

### Using groups

Groups can be used to:
- Filter stakeholder views
- Target communications to specific audiences
- Analyze impact by stakeholder segment
- Assign change impacts to multiple stakeholders at once

## Communication preferences

Track how each stakeholder prefers to receive information:

- Preferred communication channel (email, phone, in-person)
- Frequency preferences
- Best times to contact
- Language preferences

## Linking stakeholders to impacts

Connect stakeholders to the change impacts that affect them:

1. Open a change impact
2. In the **Affected stakeholders** field, select relevant stakeholders
3. Save the impact

This enables you to:
- See all impacts affecting a specific stakeholder
- Understand the change burden on individuals or groups
- Plan targeted communications and support

## Stakeholder views

### Table view

The default view presents stakeholders in a powerful data table:

- **Sortable columns**: Click headers to sort by any field
- **Filtering**: Filter by department, influence level, support level, groups, and more
- **Search**: Quick search by name or email
- **Column customization**: Show/hide columns as needed
- **Saved views**: Create and save custom filter/column configurations for quick access

#### Custom table views

Create personalized views of your stakeholder data:

1. Configure filters and column visibility
2. Click **Save view**
3. Name your view (e.g., "High influence stakeholders")
4. Access saved views from the view dropdown
5. Share views with your team or keep them personal

### Map view

Visualize stakeholder locations geographically:

- Interactive world map with stakeholder markers
- Color-coded readiness indicators
- Filter by project, department, or business unit
- Cluster markers at different zoom levels
- Click markers to view stakeholder details

<Note>
  Map view requires location data in stakeholder profiles. Enable Microsoft 365 sync or import location data via CSV.
</Note>

## Communication features

### Send email

Communicate directly with stakeholders:

1. Select stakeholders in the table
2. Click **Send email** from bulk actions
3. Compose your message
4. Preview before sending
5. Track delivery

### Microsoft Teams messages

If Microsoft Teams is connected:

1. Select stakeholders
2. Click **Send Teams message**
3. Compose and send directly via Teams

## Stakeholder reports

Generate insights about your stakeholder landscape:

- **Stakeholder map**: Geographic visualization of stakeholder locations
- **Impact report**: Which stakeholders are most affected by changes
- **Communication preferences**: Summary of contact preferences

Access reports from the **Reports** section in the sidebar.
