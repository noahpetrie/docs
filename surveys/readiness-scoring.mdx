title: Readiness scoring
description: How surveys affect stakeholder readiness levels
---

Survey responses directly influence stakeholder readiness levels in Leap Change, giving you an objective measure of change preparedness.

## How it works

When a stakeholder completes a survey:

1. **Scores are calculated** from their rating responses
2. **Readiness is determined** based on score thresholds
3. **Stakeholder profile updates** with the new readiness level

## Readiness levels

| Level | Score range | Description |
|-------|-------------|-------------|
| **Champion** | 9-10 | Actively supports and advocates |
| **Ready** | 7-8 | Prepared to adopt the change |
| **Aware** | 5-6 | Understands but needs support |
| **Resistant** | 1-4 | Skeptical or opposed |

## Calculation method

For surveys with multiple questions:

1. Average all rating question scores
2. Apply the threshold table above
3. Update the stakeholder's readiness level

### Example

A stakeholder answers three questions:
- Question 1: 7
- Question 2: 8  
- Question 3: 6

Average = (7 + 8 + 6) / 3 = 7

Result: **Ready** level

## Historical tracking

Leap Change maintains a history of readiness:

- Previous survey responses
- When readiness changed
- Trend direction (improving, declining, stable)

Access this in the stakeholder profile under **Survey Responses**.

## Manual override

You can manually set readiness levels:

1. Open the stakeholder profile
2. Click the readiness level badge
3. Select the appropriate level
4. Add a note explaining the override

Manual settings are marked and won't be automatically overwritten by future surveys.

## Using readiness data

Readiness levels help you:

- **Prioritize outreach** - Focus on resistant stakeholders
- **Segment communications** - Tailor messages to readiness level
- **Track progress** - Monitor readiness improvements over time
- **Report to leadership** - Show quantitative readiness metrics

See the [Stakeholder Impact Analysis](/reports/stakeholder-impact) report for readiness visualizations.
