Leap Change tracks stakeholder engagement to help you identify who needs attention and measure the effectiveness of your change activities.

## Engagement metrics

### Engagement score

Each stakeholder has an engagement score from 0-100, calculated from:

- Survey responses
- Communication interactions
- Meeting participation
- Activity history

### Readiness levels

Stakeholders are categorized into readiness levels:

| Level | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Champion** | Actively supports and advocates for the change |
| **Ready** | Prepared and willing to adopt the change |
| **Aware** | Understands the change but needs more support |
| **Resistant** | Skeptical or opposed to the change |

Readiness levels are updated based on survey responses and manual assessment.

### Influence and support

- **Influence** - The stakeholder's ability to affect others (Low, Medium, High)
- **Support level** - Their current stance on the change (from Resistant to Champion)

## Monitoring engagement

### Stakeholder table

Use filters in the stakeholder table to identify:

- Low engagement scores
- Resistant stakeholders
- Stakeholders who haven't been contacted recently
- High-influence stakeholders needing attention

### Reports

The [Stakeholder Impact Analysis](/reports/stakeholder-impact) report provides:

- Engagement distribution across your stakeholder population
- Readiness breakdown by department or group
- Trends over time

## Improving engagement

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Target communications">
    Use stakeholder segments to send relevant, personalized communications instead of one-size-fits-all messages.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Address concerns directly">
    Review survey responses and notes to understand specific concerns, then address them in follow-up communications.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Leverage champions">
    Identify high-influence champions and involve them in communicating the change to others.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Regular check-ins">
    Set up recurring surveys to track readiness changes over time.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
